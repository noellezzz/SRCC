STUDENT HANDBOOK
Rules and Regulations
CLASS ATTENDANCE
Students are expected to come to school punctually and regularly. They are also expected to attend the Flag Ceremony. A student who comes to school late will be given the appropriate sanction. An excuse letter signed by the parent/guardian must be presented to the Guidance Office before coming to the class so that the student may be given an admission slip. Prolonged absence due to illness or other medical conditions must be supported by a Medical Certificate signed by a medical professional. All latecomers are strictly required to obtain an admission slip from the Guidance Office before coming to class. The student is responsible for any quiz, test, or academic activity missed due to tardiness.

BRINGING UNSANCTIONED ITEMS
Students are not allowed to bring unnecessary items to the school premises and/or inside the classroom. Items such as toys, board games, musical instruments and other expensive equipment-unless said item/s is/are to be used in some way for a class or school-sanctioned activity are not allowed inside the school premises. Whenever necessary, the student/s bringing the said item/s must secure a letter from parent or the teacher concerned explaining the reason for the presence of the said item/s. The letter must be presented to the Guidance Office. Unnecessary item/s brought to school for the first time will be given a verbal reprimand. If bringing of necessary item/s is repeated, said items will be confiscated and the student's parents and/or guardians can claim them from the Guidance Office or returned to the owners only at the end of the School Year

GROOMING
Complete prescribed uniform, neat and pressed, at all times For special events or programs where students are allowed to come in civilian attire, students are enjoined to observe modesty and propriety when deciding on what is appropriate to wear within the school premises; No sleeveless tops, no plunging necklines or halter-tops, spaghetti-strapped tank tops, no body-hugging, see-through or revealing tops; No skimpy skirts or miniskirts or shorts; Male students must sport neat-looking hairstyles. Pony tails, spiked-up or unruly hairstyles are not allowed Dyed or colored hair and/or unusual markings on hair (patterned shave) are not allowed for all the members of the student body Female students are discouraged from wearing rouge, nail polish, lipstick or other forms of make up within the school premises unless for a school-sanctioned activity Students are discouraged from wearing jewelry except for a simple's wristwatch and small discreet earrings (female students only); bangles, ballers, bracelets and necklaces are also highly discouraged Male students are not allowed to wear earrings within school premises Students who sport tattoos or piercings placed other than earlobe are not allowed inside the school premises

DRESS CODE
Students are expected to come to school in COMPLETE and NEAT uniform for it serves the primary purpose of identification of the school to which said students belong to and therefore should be worn with dignity and pride. Students are reminded that P.E Uniforms must ONLY be worn during MAPEH classes/periods. All students must wear their school IDs with prescribed ID laces whenever inside the school or whenever the students are participating in school sanctioned activities inside and outside of the school premises.

USE OF SCHOOL FACILITIES
Students shall keep the school clean by refraining from spitting, littering, damaging and/or defacing school property, and by exercising CLAYGO (Clean As You Go.) Students shall at all times exercise due diligence in the care of their books, library books, laboratory equipment, computer terminals and the like.

LEAVE FROM SCHOOL DURING SCHOOL HOURS
A student is NOT allowed to leave the school during school hours. Should there be a reasonable cause for the student to exit the school premises, the student must present a letter duly signed by their parent/guardian detailing the reason for such action. The student must also be fetched by the latter's signee or a trusted proxy. In cases of sickness or other medical emergencies, a guardian will be contacted by the school.
